> Here, you can freely share your personal opinions about your experience participating in the First Cloud AI Journey (FCAJ) program at Amazon Web Services Vietnam. This will help the FCAJ team improve any shortcomings based on the following aspects:

### Overall Evaluation

The working environment is very friendly and open. FCAJ members are always willing to help whenever I encounter difficulties, even outside working hours. The workspace at the AWS office is tidy and comfortable, helping me focus better. However, I think it would be nice to have more social gatherings or team bonding activities to strengthen relationships.

My mentor at AWS provides very detailed guidance, explains clearly when I don't understand, and always encourages me to ask questions. The admin team supports administrative tasks, provides necessary documents, and creates favorable conditions for me to work effectively. I especially appreciate that the mentor allows me to try and solve problems myself instead of just giving the answer — this was especially valuable while working on the SmartCV project's AI/Bedrock integration.

The tasks I was assigned align well with the knowledge I learned at university, while also introducing me to new areas I had never encountered before, such as building serverless architectures and integrating generative AI (Amazon Bedrock) into a real product. This allowed me to both strengthen my foundational knowledge and gain practical, in-demand skills.

During the internship, I learned many new skills such as using project management tools, teamwork skills, and professional communication in a corporate environment. I also gained hands-on experience with a wide range of AWS services and automated testing practices. The mentor also shared valuable real-world experiences that helped me better plan my career path.

The company culture is very positive: everyone respects each other, works seriously but still keeps things enjoyable. When there are urgent deadlines, everyone works together and supports one another regardless of their role. This made me feel like a real part of the team, even as an intern.

The internship program does not provide an allowance; however, I was given flexible working hours when needed and the opportunity to join internal training sessions — which was still a big plus in terms of knowledge and real-world experience.

---

### Additional Questions

What I found most satisfying was applying my knowledge directly to a real product (SmartCV) instead of just studying theory — from integrating Amazon Bedrock for the AI coaching feature to building an automated test suite with high coverage. Seeing something I helped build actually running on AWS was a memorable experience.

I think the program could improve by organizing more social/team-building activities between intern groups, and by considering adding an internship allowance to help cover some of the commuting and meal costs for students.

Yes, I would still recommend it. The program has a clear learning path, supportive mentors, and gives interns the chance to work with real AWS services as well as take part in a full team project — a valuable experience despite the lack of an allowance.

---

### Suggestions & Expectations

I'd suggest adding more experience-sharing sessions from AWS staff or partner companies, and providing some form of allowance or cost support (transportation, meals) for interns working on-site at the office.

Yes, given the opportunity, I would like to continue working on AWS- and AI-related projects, particularly developing SmartCV further or building similar serverless products.

Overall, this was a valuable internship that helped me grow both professionally and in terms of teamwork skills. I want to thank the FCAJ team and my mentor for their support throughout the internship.
